## What’s new and why it matters
The most important question in any investment decision is not "which stock to buy" — it's how much of your wealth should be in stocks . This decision (asset allocation) explains most of the variation in returns between investors over the long term. Brinson, Hood and Beebower (1986) showed that over 90% of the return variability of institutional funds comes from the allocation decision, not from selection within each asset class.
