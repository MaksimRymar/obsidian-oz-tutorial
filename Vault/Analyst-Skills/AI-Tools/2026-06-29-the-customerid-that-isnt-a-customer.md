## What’s new and why it matters
An error is the safe outcome. The dangerous one is a number. When a text-to-SQL feature breaks the way you hope, it throws a missing column and dies on the screen. You see it, you fix it, nobody was misled. The failure that should keep you up is the other one. The query runs, returns rows, and hands a real user a number that is wrong, at the moment the stakes are highest. Nothing errored. Just a plausible figure, formatted to two decimals, in front of the person least able to know it's off.
