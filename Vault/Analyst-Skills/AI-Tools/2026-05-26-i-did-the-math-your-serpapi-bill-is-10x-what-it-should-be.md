## What’s new and why it matters
A few weeks ago, someone on dev.to laid it out plain: SerpAPI costs $500/month for the same data you can scrape yourself for $3. The post blew up because every developer who's ever paid for a SERP API felt that pain. But here's the part nobody talks about: building your own scraper for $3/month means owning a maintenance nightmare. Proxies rotate, CAPTCHAs evolve, Google changes its DOM weekly. That $3/month stack becomes 4 hours of debugging at 2 AM when your rank tracker silently breaks. So the real choice isn't "SerpAPI vs. DIY." It's "pay too much" vs. "build too much."
