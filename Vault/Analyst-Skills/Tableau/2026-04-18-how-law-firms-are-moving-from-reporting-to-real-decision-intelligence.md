## What’s new and why it matters
For years, law firms have relied on reporting tools to monitor performance. These tools provide useful summaries, but they often fall short when it comes to guiding decisions. The reason is simple: reporting shows what happened, not what to do next. As firms grow, this limitation becomes more visible. More matters, more clients, and more financial complexity make it harder to rely on static reports or manual analysis.
